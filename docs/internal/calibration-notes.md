# Calibration Notes

Guidelines for training, onboarding, and maintaining consistency across EC teams.

:::caution Internal Only
These calibration procedures and standards are for internal team use only.
:::

## Purpose of Calibration

**Calibration** ensures that all ECs (Submitters, Reviewers, Adjudicators) apply guidelines consistently and understand quality standards.

### Goals:
- **Consistency** - All ECs interpret guidelines the same way
- **Quality** - Everyone understands and meets the quality bar
- **Alignment** - Decisions are predictable and fair
- **Learning** - Continuous improvement through feedback

## Calibration Schedule

### Initial Onboarding
- **Week 1:** Role overview, guideline review, example walkthroughs
- **Week 2:** Practice submissions/reviews with feedback
- **Week 3:** Supervised work with real submissions
- **Week 4:** Independent work with spot-check oversight

### Ongoing Calibration
- **Weekly:** Team calibration sessions (1 hour)
- **Monthly:** Cross-team alignment meetings
- **Quarterly:** Comprehensive guideline review

## Calibration Session Structure

### Weekly Sessions (1 hour)

**Format:**
1. **Review Recent Cases** (20 min)
   - 2-3 challenging submissions from past week
   - Discuss different interpretations
   - Reach consensus on handling

2. **Guideline Discussion** (20 min)
   - Clarify ambiguous areas
   - Review common mistakes
   - Update calibration examples

3. **Metrics Review** (10 min)
   - Team consistency scores
   - Quality trends
   - Individual feedback (private)

4. **Q&A and Feedback** (10 min)
   - Open questions
   - Process improvements
   - Upcoming changes

### Example Discussion Topics

- Borderline quality submissions
- Novel failure modes
- Tool usage edge cases
- Multi-turn conversation complexity
- Reviewer feedback quality

## Calibration Materials

### Standard Examples Library

Maintain a library of calibration examples:

**Category A: Clear Accept**
- Obviously meets all criteria
- High quality, no issues
- Use to establish quality bar ceiling

**Category B: Clear Reject**
- Obviously below standards
- Multiple critical issues
- Use to establish quality bar floor

**Category C: Borderline Cases**
- Could go either way
- Requires judgment and discussion
- Use to align on edge cases

**Category D: Novel Scenarios**
- Not covered in guidelines
- Requires interpretation
- Use to set precedents

### Calibration Metrics

Track these metrics per EC:

**Agreement Rate:**
- % alignment with consensus decisions
- Target: >85% for reviewers, >90% for adjudicators

**Quality Scores:**
- Average quality of output (submissions or reviews)
- Target: >4.0/5.0

**Speed:**
- Time per task (submission/review)
- Balance with quality

**Feedback Quality:**
- Clarity and helpfulness of feedback
- Target: >4.0/5.0 from submitters

## Role-Specific Calibration

### Submitter Calibration

**Focus Areas:**
- Intent clarity and scoping
- Failure mode identification
- Good vs. bad response contrast
- Realistic prompt creation

**Practice Exercises:**
- Create submission for given failure mode
- Identify issues in sample submissions
- Revise rejected submissions

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Consistently vague intents
- Unrealistic failure modes
- Poor good/bad contrast
- Over-complicated scenarios

### Reviewer Calibration

**Focus Areas:**
- Rubric application
- Feedback quality
- Edge case handling
- Escalation judgment

**Practice Exercises:**
- Review and provide feedback on samples
- Compare reviews across reviewers
- Practice escalation decisions

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Too lenient or too strict
- Vague feedback
- Missing critical issues
- Inappropriate escalations

### Adjudicator Calibration

**Focus Areas:**
- Tie-breaking principles
- Precedent setting
- Clear decision communication
- Guideline interpretation

**Practice Exercises:**
- Resolve sample disagreements
- Create precedent documentation
- Interpret ambiguous guidelines

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Inconsistent rulings
- Unclear reasoning
- Avoiding difficult decisions
- Not documenting precedents

## Measuring Calibration Success

### Team-Level Metrics

**Inter-Rater Reliability (IRR):**
- Measure agreement between reviewers on same submissions
- Target: >0.80 (Cohen's Kappa)

**Consistency Score:**
- % of cases where team reaches consensus
- Target: >90%

**Calibration Velocity:**
- How quickly new ECs reach target consistency
- Target: <4 weeks to 80% consistency

### Individual Assessment

**Calibration Checkpoints:**

**Week 2:**
- Review 10 test submissions
- Provide feedback on 5 samples
- Target: 70% agreement with gold standard

**Week 4:**
- Full role responsibilities with oversight
- Target: 80% agreement

**Week 8:**
- Independent work
- Target: 85% agreement

**Ongoing:**
- Quarterly recalibration
- Target: Maintain 85%+ agreement

## Common Calibration Challenges

### Challenge 1: Borderline Quality Submissions

**Issue:** Team disagrees on accept/reject for borderline cases.

**Solution:**
- Use tie-breaking principles consistently
- Document precedents
- Create more borderline examples
- Regular discussion of edge cases

### Challenge 2: Inconsistent Feedback Quality

**Issue:** Reviewers provide varying quality of feedback.

**Solution:**
- Feedback templates and examples
- Peer review of feedback
- Specific feedback training
- Recognition for excellent feedback

### Challenge 3: Drift Over Time

**Issue:** Standards gradually shift as team makes incremental decisions.

**Solution:**
- Regular recalibration to gold standards
- Quarterly guideline review
- Track metrics over time
- Adjust when drift detected

### Challenge 4: New Failure Mode Patterns

**Issue:** Novel scenarios not covered in guidelines.

**Solution:**
- Rapid adjudication and documentation
- Guideline updates within 1 week
- Calibration session coverage
- Example library updates

## Calibration Best Practices

### Do ✅
- Use real examples from actual submissions
- Encourage open discussion and disagreement
- Document decisions and reasoning
- Update guidelines based on calibration insights
- Provide individual feedback privately
- Celebrate consistency improvements
- Make calibration engaging and interactive

### Don't ❌
- Make ECs feel judged or criticized
- Rush through complex cases
- Ignore minority opinions
- Skip documentation of precedents
- Overlook process improvements
- Let standards drift
- Miss calibration sessions
